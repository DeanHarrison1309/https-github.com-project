1.7 Legal Guidelines and Constraints

There are various legal issues you must take into consideration when developing a website.

Domain Name:  Domain name trademark infringement occurs when a person or business uses a domain name that is protected by a trademark, thereby infringing upon another’s trademark protection. (https://www.upcounsel.com/domain-name-trademark-infringement).

Copyright Infringement: All websites are protected by copyright.   Permission to use images or copy information from another site must be granted by the owner.

Data Protection (General Data Protection Regulations): There are many rules and regulations where the collection of personal data is involved.   .  You must have a valid reason as to why you need the information and how you intend to use it, making this very clear to the person in question.  Their information can be used for nothing other than the reason you have specified.    For a full list of all data protection regulations, visit the Information Commissioner's Office website https://ico.org.uk/.

Identifying Yourself: A business website must display all of the following.

  • Company name.
  • Registered number.
  • Registered office address.
  • Contact details, including an email address.
  • Details of how to contact business by non-electronic means.
  • The VAT number of business, if applicable.
  • Details of any trade body or regulator registration.
    •  

For sole traders and partnership, you must display the address of the principle place of business. (https://www.nibusinessinfo.co.uk/content/business-websites-legal-requirements)

Accessibility: Disabled people should be able to access and use your website.   Take into consideration things like flashing lights which may cause epileptic seizures.
